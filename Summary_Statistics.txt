Measures of Center
	|--> Mean
	|--> Median
	|--> Mode
	
Mean: Sum of the data points / Number of data points	

Data points:
9 5 3 6 11 5 17 12 4 2

Mean: 74 / 10 --> 7.4

Median: Middle value in an Ordered dataset
2 3 4 5 5 6 9 11 12 17

In Odd number of data points, is it very easy to pick the Median (Middle value)
In even number of data points, take the Mean of the 2 Middle values

Median --> (5 + 6) / 2 --> 5.5

50% of data points are lower than Median
50% of data points are higher than Median

Mode: Data point that occurs maximum of times
Here, the Mode is 5

If all the data points appear only once --> No Mode scenario
If multiple data points appear same number of times --> Multi Mode scenario

Skew: Measure of the Symmetry of the distribution

Skew is 0   --> Symmetrical distribution
Skew is > 0 --> Positive skewed (Right tail)
Skew is < 0 --> Negative skewed (Left tail)

Measures of Spread
	|--> Range
	|--> Variance
	|--> Standard Deviation
	|--> Outliers
	
Range == Min and Max	

Variance: How much on average do the data points differ from the Mean

dp1 minus Mean
dp2 minus Mean
dp3 minus Mean

Avg of (Squared distances of each data point from the Mean)

Low Variance: Data points are close to the Mean
High Variance: Data points are spread out (far) from the Mean

In Machine Learning, high variance will be hard to Model
Solution:
1) Find and eliminate the Outliers
2) Apply feature scaling using Standardization

Standard Deviation: Square root of the Variance
Tell us how much each data point on average deviates from the Mean

Outliers:
1) Data points which are significantly different from rest of the data points
2) They are either much higher or much lower then the majority of the data points

How to identify the Outliers?

1) Find out the Median

50% of data points are lower than Median
50% of data points are higher than Median

2 3 4 5 5 6 9 11 12 17
Median --> (5 + 6) / 2 --> 5.5

Q1 is the Median of the Lower half 
Q3 is the Median of the Upper half 

Q1: 4
Q3: 11

IQR: Q3 - Q1
IQR: 11 - 4 ==> 7

Lower bound: Q1 - 1.5 x IQR
Upper bound: Q3 + 1.5 x IQR

Lower bound: 4 - 10.5 ==> -6.5
Upper bound: 11 + 10.5 ==> 21.5

Any value either less than Lower bound or any value more than Upper bound is an Outlier
